# Case Study – WannaCry Ransomware Attack

One of the most famous ransomware attacks in history is the WannaCry attack, which began on 12 May 2017. It rapidly spread across the world and affected computers in more than 150 countries.

The attack targeted systems running Microsoft Windows by exploiting a security vulnerability. Once a computer was infected, the ransomware encrypted all files and displayed a message demanding a payment of around $300 in Bitcoin to restore access.

WannaCry spread very quickly and infected over 200,000 systems globally, including hospitals, companies, and government organizations. One of the major victims was the UK’s National Health Service (NHS), where thousands of appointments were cancelled due to system failures.

The attack mainly affected systems that had not been updated with the latest security patches. Even though a fix was already available, many users had not installed it, making them vulnerable.

This incident showed how dangerous ransomware can be, as it caused massive disruption and financial losses worldwide. It also highlighted the importance of regular software updates, cybersecurity awareness, and proper data backup to prevent such attacks.
